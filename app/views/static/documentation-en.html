Documentation

The categories used by the Digital Library of Novohispanic Thought are a specific application of the TEI Lite version that can be found in the following link.

The decision to use this version of TEI, and not the broader one offered by the Text Encoding Initiative, is based on two reasons. Firstly, it presents a simplified TEI version that is enough to cover all the needs of the Library. Moreover, it does so in such way that it illustrates the text markup process with enough clarity to be followed by those who don’t have a background in TEI and the markup process.

Its open structure and the possibility to add different types of attributes to the categories enable the generation of the necessary variants to practically mark everything we wanted to inside the texts.

In the first part of this document, you will be able to find the explanation of the TEI and TEI Lite elements that are used in the Library. In the second part, you will be able to find the description of the attributes that were created to markup words or specific functions within the texts in the Library.

TEI and TEI Lite elements

The markup of any text of the library starts with the specification that it is a TEI document in Spanish. To do this, we use the following reference:

  • <TEI xmlns="http://www.tei-c.org/ns/1.0" xmlns:rng="http://relaxng.org/ns/structure/1.0" xml:lang="es">
1.- Schema

Afterwards, it is necessary to add the schema that will validate the document in XML. To read about the functions and characteristics of this schema, you can go to http://www.tei-c.org/release/doc/tei-p5-doc/en/html/ST.html#STIN

In the Library we use the “TEI-minimal” schema, which covers all the categories that TEI Lite uses.

  • <schemaSpec ident="TEI-minimal" start="TEI">
  • <moduleRef key="tei"/>
  • <moduleRef key="header"/>
  • <moduleRef key="core"/>
  • <moduleRef key="textstructure"/>
  • <moduleRef key="namesdates"/>
  • </schemaSpec>
2.- TEI Header

After specifying the schema, we must specify the TEI Header, whose functions and qualities can be read about on http://www.tei-c.org/release/doc/tei-p5-doc/en/html/HD.html.

El Header que presentamos a continuación corresponde al utilizado en la Especulación Astrológica y Física

  • <distributor>Biblioteca Digital del Pensamiento Novohispano</distributor>
  • <respStmt>

The categories that are used within the Header are the following:

  • <teiHeader></teiHeader> specifies that the code is referring to the Header of the document.
  • <fileDesc> </fileDesc> specifies the description of the document.
  • <titleStmt></titleStmt> (title statement) specifies the title of the work, and the names of those who are responsible for its intellectual content.
  • <title></title> specifies the title of the work. This one is only used in the case of the title of the marked work, and not in the one of the rest of the evoked titles of the work. To refer to those, we use the category <refTitle></refTitle>.
  • <author></author> specifies the author of the work. This is used only to refer to the author of the marked work.
  • <publicationStmt></publicationStmt> (publication statement) specifies information about both the digital publishing and the publishing in print of the marked work.
  • <publisher></publisher> refers to the publisher of the digital edition. In all cases, this will be the National Autonomous University of Mexico, Faculty of Philosophy and Literature.
  • <distributor></distributor> refers to the distributor of the work. In all cases, this will always be the Digital Library of Novohispanic Thought.
  • <respStmt></respStmt> refers to the responsibility behind the publication.
  • <name></name> establishes the name of the person that is responsible for the publication.
  • <resp></resp> specifies the responsibility.
  • <sourceDesc></sourceDesc> refers to the description of the source in print of the marked text.
  • <bibl></bibl> corresponds to a bibliographical reference of the marked text. It must include the full catalographic index card of the text.
3.- Text markup

To mark up the text, the following categories are used:

a) The text <text></text> this shows the beginning and ending of the marked text.

b) The cover <front></front> this marks the cover or front of the document in print.

<titlePage></titlePage> marks the page that contains the title of the work, which may coincide with the cover.

<docTitle></docTitle> marks the title of the document as seen on the cover.

<docImprint><docImprint> marks the place and publisher of the text.

<docDate></docDate> marks the date of the text.

c) The body of the document

<body></body> corresponds to the body of the text in its entirety.

<p></p> is a central category. It marks the beginning and ending of each paragraph. On the documents of the library, when paragraphs don’t end within the same page they started in, it is still necessary to use this tool to mark the ending of the paragraph on the first page, right before the inclusion of the </div> command. A new paragraph must be marked in the following page, after <div>, to complete the paragraph.

<div></div> marks the start of a division. In the library, it is used to separate texts into folia. Folia are, along with the paragraphs, the reference unit in the texts of this library.

<term></term> marks a word, a group of words, a symbolic designation or the use of a technical term. It is a central markup category, as it allows for the identification of words and expressions that are used in the text and whose study and recovery are relevant. This command accepts 7 Type attributes: medical (Med), astrological (Astro), astronomical (Astron), philosophical (Philo), mathematical (Mathe), theological (Theolo) and Old Spanish (OldSp). In all cases, a “reg” may be included to unify terms using modern spelling as a basis. This is especially important in the case of old Spanish words, in which the use of “reg” is compulsory.

Examples:

  • <term type="astro" Reg="Juicio">juizio</term>
  • <term type="astro" Reg="Venéreo">Venereo</term>
  • <term type="med">peste</term> (this doesn’t need “reg" as there is no need for the standardization of the spelling of the word)

<foreign></foreign> identifies terms or groups of terms in a language that is different to the one the text was written in. It accepts the lang attribute (language). For the most commonly occurring language, Latin, we use the lat attribute.

Examples:

  • <foreign lang="lat">Laus Deo</foreign>

<date></date> allows us to mark dates on the text. When we find expressions such as “the year of the Lord 1654” or “the year of 1204”, we will only mark the number. When we find expressions such as “the past year”, or “two years ago”, we will mark the entire expression, but a “reg” attribute must be used too to mark the year in number format. When the expression of the date includes days and/or months, these elements will be marked, too.

Examples:

  • Este año de <date>1682</date>
  • …año del Señor de <date>396</date>

As most comets mentioned in the texts were named in regard to the dates they were seen, we will use “type” and “comet” to mark this information.

Examples:

  • como el del año del señor de <date type="comet">396</date>

<abbr></abbr>marks an abbreviation. Abbreviations are not regularized nor expanded in the mark-up; in other words, they are marked up just as they appear in the text.

  • <abbr>Vmd.</abbr>
  • <abbr>N.S.R.E.</abbr>

<pb></pb> marks the number of the folia or of the page, if this information is available in the text.

<name></name> marks the proper names of people, gods, and places; it accepts the use of the “type” attribute along with the following: person, planet, place, inst (institution), constellation, zodiacal, CathDiv (catholic divinity). Also, “reg” is used to standardize names according to their contemporary usage.

Examples:

  • <name type="person" reg="Aristóteles">Aristoteles</name>
  • <name type="constelation"">Capricorno</name>
  • <name type="planet">Saturno</name> En todos los casos se marcan como planeta Luna y Sol, además del resto de los planetas mencionados
  • <name type="place" reg="Holanda">Olanda</name>

<q></q> identifies the textual citing that appears within a text and in which bibliographical references don’t occur. These attributes must be placed at the beginning and at the ending of each quotation.

<cit></cit> is used to mark the title of a work that is mentioned within a text. In the marking of these titles, “reg” may be used to make a standardization based on the modern name of the work.

<refTitle></refTitle> Se utiliza para marcar el titulo de una obra dentro de un texto. Acepta reg para estandarizar el nombre moderno de la obra.

<head></head> is used to identify subtitles in the text.

4.- Label attributes

Reg: this attribute allows us to standardize names and other terms. It can also be used in the following cases:

  1. When a divided word needs to be marked in the text (e.g. Agus-tín), “reg” must be included along with the full undivided name in quotes.
  2. When quotations are longer than one line, “reg” must be included along with the full quotation in the way it should appear when this information is retrieved.
  3. The standardizations of terms in plural, when “reg” is added, should be done using the singular form of the word.

Type: this establishes attributes that are specific to a term or to a word, and it is used to identify with precision what class of name or term is the one that has been marked. See examples of the usage of this attribute in the cases of names and terms.

5.- Name standardization

For the standardization of names, we are using the following criteria:

Proper names:

Surname and name. “De”, “von”, and other prepositions that appear in names will appear between brackets after the surname. If a reference is being made to a saint, “san” will be placed before the name. The contemporary spelling of the name will always be used.

  • Hipona (de), San Agustín
  • Aquino (de), Santo Tomás
  • Escobar Salmerón y Castro, José (de)
  • Aristóteles
  • Séneca
  • Júpiter
  • Real Universidad se regulariza siempre como Real y Pontificia Universidad de México a menos que se trate de otra universidad.
  • Febo y Phoebo se regularizan siempre como Apolo
  • Ptolomeo se regulariza como Ptolomeo, Claudio

In the case of names of works or of institutions, they will be standardized according to the name with which they are currently known.

With few exceptions, plurals will be standardized in their singular forms; conjugated verbs will be standardized in their infinitive form.

6.- Criteria to markup words in old Spanish <OldSp>
  1. First, it is necessary to read the full text to become familiar with the vocabulary that is used by each author.
  2. For the selection of words to be marked up, it is possible to select words that don’t seem common in our everyday language.
  3. After that, it is possible to look up the word in the Diccionario de la Real Academia Española (which may be found online); if this word doesn’t appear in the dictionary, it must be marked in the text. If it does exist, the author of the markup must evaluate the importance of the word, considering the frequency of its use today.
  4. To know if the word is commonly used or not, it may be searched for on ‘Google’ to find the contexts of its use.

The uses of “u” and “v” must be respected as they appear in the original version of each text.

7.- Criteria to markup astrological or astronomical words

Even if the following criteria aren’t precise, we will use the tag <astro> (for “astrological”) to mark all terms that aren’t used by contemporary astronomy to characterize celestial phenomena, such as the terms that describe types of comets or the definition of one of their attributes. We will only use the tag <astron> (for “astronomical”) on terms that are currently used by astronomy; for example, terms such as eclipse, cometa (comet), paralaje (parallax), meteoros (meteors) will be tagged as astronomical in the following manner:

  • <term type="Astron">Cometa</term>

In the case of stars, a reference may be made to the name of each:

  • la <term type="Astro">Estrella <foreing lang="lat">Capud Medusae</foreing></term>, en que feneciò el Cometa

However, if the term is not used by contemporary astronomical science, and it instead makes a reference to the division of cosmos, it will be tagged as astrological; for example, región de fuego, juicio del año, conjunción magna; will be marked up as follows:

  • <term type="Astro" reg="Región de fuego">region de fuego</term>

Terms that may need to be tagged in their full version may exist, especially among astrological terms. For example:

  • <term type="Astro">Cometa Sublunar</term>
8.- Criteria to markup allusions

Allusions, such as El filósofo, el profeta, et cetera, are marked as names, with person types, and standardized with the name of the person that is referred to in the text. For example:

  • como señal <name type="peroson" ref="Aristóteles">el filósofo</name> en Metafísica.

There is a quotation in Latin, tagged with <foreign lang="Lat"></foreign>, but the text doesn’t say where it was taken from. In this case, since we don’t know what the source is, we will tag it with “reg” within the <q> label as a modern reference (including the author, work, and specific part of the work). If this information is unknown, then the quotation will be tagged with <q> but without the “reg· attribute.

10.- Use of the subtitles label:

<head></head>

If there is more than one level in the usage of subtitles within a text, the following label should be used:

  • <head type ="subtitle1"></head>

The number in this label will depend on each level, from top to bottom.

  • <head type ="subtitle2"></head>

The citation of the work should be marked with the <bibl> label, and it should include the author, title and publishing information. The title will be tagged with <abrTitle></abrTitle> to abbreviate it, and make sure that only a fragment of it will appear.
